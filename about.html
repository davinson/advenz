MSRS

Our path to wireless power you can depend on

Aaron, Phyo, Grayson, and Charlie were brought together by a passion for power electronics and a desire to make the world a better place, first overlapping at a conference in Italy.

On the conference floor, they dreamed of how high-frequency power electronics and incredible electromagnetics could transform wireless power and accelerate electrification.

A few months later, Resonant Link was launched around a revolutionary way to send wireless power that was 5x faster than anything else available then — or since.

Our founding team was laser-focused on using wireless charging to speed the adoption of electric vehicles and address the coming global health crisis of climate change.

They also realized that wireless charging could save lives right away by eliminating the wires and battery replacement surgeries needed to power implanted medical devices, like pacemakers.

Less than a year after forming, Resonant Link partnered with their first Fortune 500 customer, who had been working on wireless power for half a century, to develop non-invasive wireless power for a life-saving medical device. With this first success, Resonant Link started on the path to center electricity for human health, inside and out.

Today, the Resonant Link team delivers 5x faster and highly-efficient wireless power for applications big to small, from massive industrial trucks to medical devices smaller than a fingernail.

Resonant Link is anchored by offices in South Burlington, VT, Zurich, CH, and Boston, MA, and has amassed double-digit customers in healthcare, electric vehicles, and consumer electronics.

With backing from top investors and government agencies, they are poised to continue their exponential growth and lead the movement to electrify work, logistics, transportation, and patient care to help people, businesses, and the planet thrive.

Aaron Stein, PhD

Chief Technology Officer

Aaron Stein received a B.S. in Engineering from Swarthmore College before getting his M.S. and Ph.D. in Power Electronics from the University of Michigan. Aaron joined Professor Charles Sullivan’s lab at the Thayer School of Engineering at Dartmouth College as a postdoctoral fellow and, together with Phyo, developed a revolutionary wireless power transfer system using a first-of-its-kind multi-layer self-resonant structure (MSRS), an innovation that won APEC’s Best Paper Award in 2017.

Aaron was named on Business Insider’s Power Players list, which features the 30 leaders most likely to usher the automotive industry into its electrified future.

Grayson Zulauf, PhD

Chief Executive Officer

Grayson Zulauf received the B.A. and B.Eng. degrees from Dartmouth College and the M.S. and Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Stanford University, with a focus on wide-bandgap semiconductors for next-generation power electronics. Grayson dedicated his early career to electrifying commercial transportation at Motiv Power Systems as an Electrical Engineer and Product Manager.

Grayson has received multiple awards and fellowships, including the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship, Forbes 30 Under 30 Energy, and the 2022 Dial Fellowship.

Phyo Aung Kyaw, PhD

Chief Science Officer

Phyo Aung Kyaw received a B.A. in Physics from Amherst College, and a Ph.D. in Engineering Sciences (Electrical Engineering) from the Thayer School of Engineering at Dartmouth College, where his research focused on improving the efficiency and compactness of next-generation higher-frequency power electronics.

Together with Aaron, he developed the first multi-layer self-resonant structure (MSRS) prototype, which is the cornerstone of Resonant Link’s wireless power system that won the 2017 APEC’s Best Paper Award. In 2021, Phyo was recognized as a Forbes 30 Under 30 for being one of the leading entrepreneurs working to change the world.
